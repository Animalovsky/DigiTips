Accomodation

Napier start taking applications in May for September entry of that year and in November for January starts. This is safe, usually centrally located, self-catering accommodation. The online system goes live in May 2016 here. You will need an unconditional offer, email address and your Edinburgh Napier number.

Accommodation is single bedroom around £500 - £650 a month in a shared flat with 5-6 other people with a common room and kitchen. You are allowed guests, whom you are responsible for, but you are not allowed overnight guests. Smoking is not allowed on premises (neither out of windows) and this includes the use of electronic cigarettes. You can read more in the specimen tenancy here. Flats are filled by napier allocation policy where they match people of similar age group and study need. You can read the whole policy file here.

Additional needs

There is a number of accessible flats. If you have any additional needs contact the office before making an application. You can contact napier accommodation for both private and university accommodation at 01314553713 accommodation@napier.ac.uk.

Lots of students look for accommodation privately. The cost and quality can vary – you can rent a fully furnished one-bedroom flat from £450 (bills not included but as a student you are exempt of a council tax) or rent a room in a shared 3, 4 or 5 bedroom flat located in tenement style building which can cost between £280 - £400 a month. Never pay a deposit before seeing the flat! The University accommodation team provides support with finding private accommodation also.

If you decide to rent on your own or rent the whole flat with your friends, keep in mind that there is lots of people looking for flats. The best way is to search for flats you like and arrange the viewings as soon as possible – call the agencies as soon as they open. If you like the flat – go to the agency as soon as you can with deposit ready, if you call the next day, flat might be already taken.
