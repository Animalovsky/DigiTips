Tips and Tricks

Accomodation

Napier starts taking applications in May for September entry of that year and in November for January starts. This is safe, usually centrally located, self-catering accommodation. The online system goes live in May 2016 here. You will need an unconditional offer, email address and your Edinburgh Napier number.

READ MORE

Funding

To register for funding such as student loan go to SAAS and click on login in at the top right corner where you can register your details. You must apply for SAAS every year you are studying if you want help with paying your fees. If you decide to study in Edinburgh, you have a big chance to be eligible for funding.

READ MORE

Student Discounts

As a student you can buy a NUS card to get lots of discounts such as contact lenses, eating out, entertainment, cinema, Spotify, fashion, finance, gym, music, supermarkets, Railcard, broadband, uni essentials and many more. To buy a card just enter your email address at Nus Extra and in the place of study add Edinburgh Napier University Students' Association for Napier student or Edinburgh Col- lege Students' Association for Edinburgh college students.

Student Nightlife

For a cheap start of the night hit Bar Salsa on cowgate. It’s very cheap and busy. You can then follow to many of the clubs on cowgate – The Bongoclub, Sneaky Pete’s, The cabaret Voltaire, The Liquid rooms, The La Belle Angele and many more. We recommend sneakers to those clubs! If you want to dress up hit the George street, El Barrio, Lulu or Shanghai. The favorite student pubs are Malones and The Three sisters.

Referencing

Writing assignments at university will be in form of essay, reports or papers and dissertation in the last years of an undergraduate programme. There is a whole page on mynapier to help you with writing but the most useful tools for us were academic phrase-bank and Mendeley.

READ MORE

Council Tax

As a full-time student you probably wont have to pay your Council Tax bill. To qualify as a student, you must either: • study for 24 weeks for 21 hours per week in each academic year • be under 20 years old, have left home and be studying for a qualifying course for at least 12 hours a week for three months - qualifying courses include A levels, Highers, HNDs, HNCs and Level 4 SVQs
